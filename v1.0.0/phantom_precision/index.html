What this wedge tests

We give the model a paragraph of plausible business prose. One numerical claim in the paragraph is fabricated. One to three are real and sourced. The model is asked to flag the fabricated claim. We score per-passage F1 against ground-truth fabricated spans, with the distractor false-positive rate reported separately.

Pre-registered on 2026-05-21 by VALIS Systems. Roster: 3 frontier models.

Model comparison

What the numbers mean

Claude Opus 4.7 found the fabricated statistic in 99% of cases. GPT-5.5 found it in 99% of cases. Gemini 3.1 Pro found it 100% of the time it got to produce an answer at all.

The catch is what they did next. Recall on the fabricated claim is near-perfect across all three models. Precision is not. The macro F1 numbers (Opus 0.63, GPT 0.65, Gemini 0.24) reflect a single shared behavior: when the model is asked to flag the fake statistic in a paragraph that also contains real, sourced statistics, it tends to flag the real ones too. Models are cautious. They over-cite.

This is a quieter failure than "the model hallucinated a number." It means a model deployed to vet other models' outputs would generate a high false-positive rate on legitimate citations. The verification layer is itself unreliable.

Gemini's column reads worse for a different reason. It hits the 1024 max_tokens budget on 54% of cases. Its internal thinking consumes the budget before the answer arrives. On the cases it did finish (228 of 500), it scored similar precision-recall behavior to Opus and GPT.

What would falsify this finding: a frontier model that finds the fabricated claim while also recognizing that "47% of Fortune 500 companies report X" (a real, sourced statistic) is not a hallucination. None of the three models tested do that consistently at v1.0.0.

Full methodology & rubric

Scoring

Scorer: phantom_precision_v1
Metric: f1

Rubric

Per-passage F1 against the ground-truth set of fabricated quantitative spans. A true positive is a span the model flags as fabricated that matches a span in fabricated_claims under normalized exact match (strip + collapse whitespace + lowercase; no currency stripping, no unit synonymization). A false positive is a span the model flags that is not in that set (including any span listed under distractors). A false negative is a fabricated_claims span the model misses. Refusals are recorded as a separate outcome category and excluded from the F1 calculation; per-roster refusal rates and the full seven-category outcome breakdown are reported alongside the score.

Refusal policy

Refusals are scored as a separate outcome category, not as wrong answers. A refusal flag is set when the model declines to evaluate the passage; F1 is computed only over non-refused cases. Refusal rates are surfaced alongside the score so that high-refusal regimes cannot game the metric. All seven outcome categories (response, refusal, partial_response, error_timeout, error_rate_limit, error_other, malformed) are reported in the results bundle.

Failure publication commitment

Null and unfavorable results publish on the same methodology page as positive results. There is no scenario in which VALIS scores a model and withholds the finding because the result is uncomfortable. This commitment is anchored to the same Bitcoin block as the manifest.
