Scoring

Scorer: mechanism_gap_v1
Metric: binary_f1

Binary F1 with `unsupported` as the positive class. A true positive is a case the model labels `unsupported` whose ground-truth label is `unsupported`. A false positive is a model `unsupported` label on a ground-truth `supported` case. A false negative is a model `supported` label (or no parseable label) on a ground-truth `unsupported` case. Recall on `unsupported` is the headline metric (the design hypothesis is keyed to it). Refusals are recorded as a separate outcome category and routed through refused=True at the scorer; non-response outcomes other than refusal (timeouts, rate-limits, malformed responses) are routed through label=None and treated as malformed. The full seven- category outcome breakdown is reported alongside the score.

Refusal policy

Refusals are scored as a separate outcome category, not as wrong answers. Explicit refusals route through `refused=True` at the scorer; non-response outcomes other than refusal (timeout, rate-limit, malformed) route through `label=None` and are reported as malformed. All seven outcome categories (response, refusal, partial_response, error_timeout, error_rate_limit, error_other, malformed) are reported in the results bundle. Models that refuse all cases score "refusal: 100%" with no F1 reported.

Failure publication commitment

Null and unfavorable results publish on the same methodology page as positive results. There is no scenario in which VALIS scores a model and withholds the finding because the result is uncomfortable. This commitment is anchored to the same Bitcoin block as the manifest.
