What this wedge tests

We give the model a claim and ask whether the claim is falsifiable. Falsifiable claims have observable consequences if false; unfalsifiable claims do not. We score binary F1 against rule-derived ground truth, with refusal as a separate category.

Pre-registered on 2026-05-21 by VALIS Systems. Roster: 3 frontier models.

What the numbers mean

GPT-5.5 gets it right 83% of the time. Gemini 3.1 Pro 81%. Opus 4.7 74%. F1 on the "stated unfalsifiable" label: 0.85 / 0.84 / 0.79.

The wedge asks the model to read a claim and decide whether the claim could in principle be tested against reality. "Companies that prioritize customer experience grow faster" is unfalsifiable as written. "Companies with NPS above 50 grew revenue 12% YoY in 2024" is falsifiable. Models are asked to distinguish the two.

All three pass the recall test. They almost never miss an unfalsifiable claim (recall 1.0, 1.0, 0.996). What they get wrong is in the other direction: they sometimes call a precise, falsifiable claim "unfalsifiable" too. Precision is the differentiator.

Why this matters: a model deployed to flag "vague claims" in analysis would systematically over-flag falsifiable but imprecise prose. The model treats vagueness and unfalsifiability as the same thing. They are not.

What would falsify this finding: a model that hits 0.95 F1 here while maintaining precision above 0.85. The current frontier sits closer to 0.79 precision on the unfalsifiable label even when recall is maximal.

Full methodology & rubric

Scoring

Scorer: falsifier_observability_v1
Metric: binary_f1

Rubric

Binary F1 with `stated_unfalsifiable` as the positive class. A true positive is a case the model labels `stated_unfalsifiable` whose ground-truth label is `stated_unfalsifiable`. A false positive is a model `stated_unfalsifiable` label on a ground-truth `observable_falsifier` case. A false negative is a model `observable_falsifier` label (or no parseable label) on a ground-truth `stated_unfalsifiable` case. Recall on `stated_unfalsifiable` is the headline metric (the design hypothesis is keyed to it). Refusals are recorded as a separate outcome category and routed through refused=True at the scorer; non-response outcomes other than refusal (timeouts, rate-limits, malformed responses) are routed through label=None and treated as malformed. The full seven-category outcome breakdown is reported alongside the score. Rubric convention: a claim is `observable_falsifier` when, taking the sentence as written, a competent reviewer could verify its failure mode within ~90 days using only information stated in the sentence -- a concrete metric, threshold or directional outcome, and time bound. A claim is `stated_unfalsifiable` when no observation could falsify it as written, including (i) vacuous strategic affirmations, (ii) self-sealing predicates ("will adapt to whatever conditions emerge"), (iii) macro-conditional framings ("if the economy holds up", "if regulatory tailwinds continue") because no in-sentence observation verifies the macro state -- same logic as mechanism_gap's external-macro rule -- and (iv) surface-form mimicry where a number or date is bound to a decoy predicate (e.g. "directional health", "strategic positioning") so no observation could check it.

Refusal policy

Refusals are scored as a separate outcome category, not as wrong answers. Explicit refusals route through `refused=True` at the scorer; non-response outcomes other than refusal (timeout, rate-limit, malformed) route through `label=None` and are reported as malformed. All seven outcome categories (response, refusal, partial_response, error_timeout, error_rate_limit, error_other, malformed) are reported in the results bundle. Models that refuse all cases score "refusal: 100%" with no F1 reported.

Failure publication commitment

Null and unfavorable results publish on the same methodology page as positive results. There is no scenario in which VALIS scores a model and withholds the finding because the result is uncomfortable. This commitment is anchored to the same Bitcoin block as the manifest.
